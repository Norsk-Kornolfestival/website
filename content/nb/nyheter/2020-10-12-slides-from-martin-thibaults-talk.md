As promised, Martin has sent the slides for [his talk](https://www.norskkornolfestival.no/2020/10/04/talk-by-martin-thibault/) on farmhouse yeasts from the Andes, Bhutan, and Ethiopia. You can download the slides here:

[martin-thibault-talk-2020](https://www.norskkornolfestival.no/wp-content/uploads/2020/10/martin-thibault-talk-2020.pdf)[Last ned](https://www.norskkornolfestival.no/wp-content/uploads/2020/10/martin-thibault-talk-2020.pdf)

He also sent these links to the studies he mentioned in his talk:

- [The study](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/27794522_Chemical_and_nutritional_properties_of_'tej'_an_indigenous_Ethiopian_honey_wine_variations_within_and_between_production_units) where 200 Tej samples were analyzed.- Same samples, same people, [different study](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16943014/).- [On the Ethiopian beer Tella](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2352618115000426).- [On marriage customs in Bhutan](http://www.bhutanstudies.org.bt/publicationFiles/Monograph/monoMarriage-Customs3.pdf) (do a search for 'chang' and you will see many words exist in Dzongha to describe alcoholic beverages served; the word changes with the context).- [The myth behind alcohol happiness in Bhutan](http://www.gpiatlantic.org/conference/papers/dorji.pdf) (again, search for 'chang'; this text though is heavily biased and aims to make Bhutanese people realize that they are drinking too often).
