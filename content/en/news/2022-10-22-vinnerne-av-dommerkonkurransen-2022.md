The homebrews served at the festival were evaluated by a judging committee, who selected their favorites and crowned the year's Norwegian champion in corn beer brewing.

![](/images/wp/2022/10/IMG_0522-1024x683.jpg)

The committee consisted of (from left to right in the picture above):

- Per Øyvind Arnesen, chairman of [Norbryggs](https://norbrygg.no/) judging committee.- Linda van Loon, Dutch microbiologist at [Eik & Tid](https://www.eiktid.no/).- [Amund Polden Arnesen](https://no.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amund_Polden_Arnesen), brewer at Eik & Tid, Norway's first beer sommelier and responsible for training Norwegian beer sommeliers.- Dag Jørgensen, former brewer and owner at Voss Bryggeri.- Sølvi Strifeldt, [beer blogger](https://olhunnen.strifeldt.net/) and certified beer judge.- Lars Andreas Tomasgård, traditional brewer and two-time Norwegian champion in corn beer brewing.

# 3rd place: Thor Humberset

![](/images/wp/2022/10/IMG_0551-1024x683.jpg)

Thor took 3rd place for the beer "Kok", a "homebrew" (a beer in the style from Hardanger, Voss and Sogn) at 9% fermented with #1 Sigmund. Thor has had several strong placements in previous years as well.

The judges said the following: "A beer with strong caramel aroma, spiced and with some floral notes. The taste has strong sweetness and plenty of caramel flavor, and has a good bitterness. This is a good and balanced beer."

Thor won a sack of Stjørdalsmalt from Jogeir Svee Halland and a signed copy of the book [Norsk Tradisjonsbrygging](https://museumsforlaget.no/produkt/norsk-tradisjonsbrygging/), which comes out in April.

# 2nd place: Kjell Arne Bjørdal

![](/images/wp/2022/10/IMG_0559-1024x683.jpg)

Kjell Arne won second place with the beer "Smakløyså", a traditional corn ale with #41 Skare. Kjell Arne was part of Ørsta Ølbryggarlaug, who became Norwegian champions last year.

The judges said: "A beer with nice herbal character, which is delicate and balanced. It is fresh and easy-drinking with nice grain flavor. This is a very fine and fresh corn ale that represents the type well."

Kjell Arne won 2 growlers from [Brewshop](https://brewshop.no/) and a sack of malt from Voss Bryggeri.

# Norwegian Champion: Jørund Geving

![](/images/wp/2022/10/IMG_0588-1024x683.jpg)

"The Viking" became Norwegian champion for the third time with his Stjørdalsøl fermented with kveik he received from Nils Gjelland at Voss. He is the first to have become Norwegian champion three times.

The judges said: "A beautiful, copper-colored beer, which the judging panel considers to be very true to type. Aroma of strong smoke with bacon and leather, complex grain and caramel with some burnt malt, light sourness and bitterness. The beer is strong and well-balanced, something the judges would like to have more of. This is a very fine Stjørdalsøl that the brewer can be proud of."

Jørund won a rifle stock from [GRS Riflestocks](https://www.grsriflestocks.com/) in Hornindal.
